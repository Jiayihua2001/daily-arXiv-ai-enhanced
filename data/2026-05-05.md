### [2] [EZ-Therm: Effective Zero-Shot Thermal Depth Completion by Adapting Diffusion Priors](https://doi.org/10.1109/LRA.2026.3682982)
*Geonhwa Son,Taejoo Kim,Jeongmin Shin,Seunghyeon Lee,Namil Kim,Yukyung Choi*

Main category: Other

TL;DR: The paper proposes the first diffusion-based zero-shot thermal depth completion framework that adapts an RGB-trained foundation model to thermal imagery and predicts metric-scale depth in challenging visual conditions.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Depth completion is important for autonomous systems in degraded visual environments, but standard RGB-based methods fail in low light or adverse conditions. Thermal sensing is better suited for these scenarios, yet thermal depth completion has been underexplored.

Method: The framework has two main components: Thermal-Aware Multispectral Adaptation (TAMA), which uses paired RGB-thermal data to reduce the domain gap and improve thermal understanding, and Thermal-aware Proxy Alignment (TAPA), which injects thermal guidance and sparse metric constraints directly into the diffusion sampling process instead of relying on iterative optimization.

Result: The method produces reliable metric-scale depth predictions across diverse thermal scenes and achieves performance competitive with fully supervised baselines, despite being zero-shot and not requiring task-specific training.

Conclusion: The work shows that diffusion-based zero-shot transfer from RGB to thermal depth completion is feasible and effective, providing a promising foundation for robust 3D perception in degraded visual environments.

Abstract: For autonomous systems operating in degraded visual environments, robust 3D perception depends on accurate, metric-scale depth prediction. Yet, thermal depth completion—despite being well-suited for such conditions—remains relatively underexplored. Conventional depth completion approaches, which fuse sparse LiDAR with RGB images, perform well under well-lit conditions but break down in low light or adverse conditions due to the inherent limitations of RGB sensing. We present the first diffusion-based zero-shot thermal depth completion framework, extending the capability of an RGB-trained foundation model to the thermal domain. Our approach is twofold: First, to bridge the domain gap, we introduce a Thermal-Aware Multispectral Adaptation (TAMA) that leverages paired RGB-thermal images to enhance the model’s understanding of thermal image. Second, we replace the iterative optimization process with our Thermal-aware Proxy Alignment (TAPA) module, which directly integrates thermal guidance and sparse metric constraints into the diffusion sampling process. Our framework enables reliable, metric-scale depth prediction across diverse thermal scenes. Notably, our zero-shot method demonstrates performance competitive with fully-supervised baselines that require task-specific training, establishing a foundation for robust 3D perception in challenging environments.

### [7] [SemDiff: Semantic Guided Diffusion-Based Collaborative Filtering Framework](https://doi.org/10.1109/TKDE.2026.3681070)
*Xufeng Liang,Zhida Qin,Haoyan Fu,Enjun Du,Haotian He,Chong Zhang,Tianyu Huang,Gangyi Ding*

Main category: Other

TL;DR: SemDiff is a semantic-guided diffusion-based collaborative filtering framework that improves recommendation by replacing random noise with auxiliary semantic signals and by designing a collaborative training objective to reduce interference between generation and preference learning.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Existing diffusion recommender systems struggle with sparse interaction data, noisy Gaussian corruption that can hide user preferences, and conflicting optimization between reconstruction and ranking objectives. These issues limit both recommendation accuracy and generative capability.

Method: The framework injects semantic information from text or image modalities into the denoising input instead of using purely random Gaussian noise. It also introduces a collaborative training objective that aligns generation and preference learning so they reinforce each other rather than compete. In addition, multiple GCN layers are used only at inference time to capture higher-order co-occurrence signals without increasing training cost.

Result: Experiments on four real-world datasets show that SemDiff outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, indicating stronger recommendation performance and improved generative effectiveness.

Conclusion: The paper presents a practical diffusion-based recommendation paradigm that leverages multimodal semantics and coordinated optimization to address sparsity and training conflict, yielding better collaborative filtering results.

Abstract: Diffusion models have demonstrated promising potential in recommender systems owing to their powerful generative ability. However, due to the inherent sparse nature of real-world recommendation data and the inconsistency in the variation of reconstruction and ranking losses during training, existing works suffer from two issues: 1) Randomly sampled Gaussian noise addition tends to obscure original user preferences. 2) Training for generation and preference learning tasks interferes with each other, limiting the generative ability of the model. To address these issues, we propose SemDiff, a semantic guided diffusion-based collaborative filtering framework. For the first issue, instead of using random Gaussian noise, we leverage rich semantic information by incorporating auxiliary signals from text or image modalities to enhance the input data of denoising model. In response to the second issue, based on a comprehensive analysis of the mutual influence between generation and preference learning in diffusion recommender systems, we build a collaborative training objective strategy to transform the interference between them into mutual collaboration, which jointly enhances the effectiveness of model training. Additionally, we employ multiple GCN layers only during inference to incorporate higher-order co-occurrence information while maintaining training efficiency. Extensive experiments on four real-world datasets demonstrate that SemDiff significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods. Our SemDiff offers an effective solution for enhancing recommendation performance and suggests a novel paradigm for applying diffusion methods in recommender systems.
